Birdwatching

Minnesota is home to a rich variety of bird species and offers many birdwatching hotspots for enthusiasts to explore.

Sax-Zim Bog

Address: County Rd 7, Meadowlands, MN 55765
Contact Info: Email info@saxzim.org or visit www.saxzim.org

Sax-Zim Bog is a premier birding destination located in northern Minnesota. It is renowned for its boreal bird species, including the Great Gray Owl, Northern Hawk Owl, and Boreal Chickadee. The bog covers a large area of spruce, fir, and tamarack forest, interspersed with wetlands and open areas, making it a diverse habitat for birds. Visitors can explore the area on several trails and roads, and there are several bird feeders and observation decks to spot birds. The Sax-Zim Bog Visitor Center offers educational exhibits and programs.

Hawk Ridge Bird Observatory

Address: 1371 Hankes Ave, Duluth, MN 55812
Contact Info: Call (218) 428-6209 or visit www.hawkridge.org

Hawk Ridge is a world-renowned bird migration site located in Duluth, Minnesota. During the fall migration, thousands of birds of prey, including Bald Eagles, Northern Goshawks, and Sharp-shinned Hawks, pass through the area on their way to warmer climates. The observatory offers a unique opportunity to witness the impressive spectacle of raptors soaring over Lake Superior. Visitors can view the birds from several observation decks, participate in educational programs and events, and visit the interpretive center.

Minnesota Valley National Wildlife Refuge

Address: 3815 American Blvd E, Bloomington, MN 55425
Contact Info: Call (952) 854-5900 or visit www.fws.gov/refuge/minnesota_valley

The Minnesota Valley National Wildlife Refuge is a 14,000-acre protected area located along the Minnesota River, providing habitat for a diverse range of wildlife, including over 270 bird species. The refuge has several trails and observation decks that offer excellent birdwatching opportunities. Visitors can spot Bald Eagles, Sandhill Cranes, and a variety of waterfowl and songbirds. The refuge also offers educational programs and events throughout the year.

Rice Lake National Wildlife Refuge

Address: 36289 State Hwy 65, McGregor, MN 55760
Contact Info: Call (218) 768-2402 or visit www.fws.gov/refuge/rice_lake

Rice Lake National Wildlife Refuge is a 18,000-acre protected area located in northern Minnesota. It includes several lakes, wetlands, and forests, providing habitat for a variety of bird species, including waterfowl, Bald Eagles, and Sandhill Cranes. The refuge has several trails and observation decks that offer excellent birdwatching opportunities. Visitors can also fish, hunt, and participate in educational programs and events.

Mille Lacs Kathio State Park

Address: 15066 Kathio State Park Rd, Onamia, MN 56359
Contact Info: Call (320) 532-3523 or visit www.dnr.state.mn.us/state_parks/mille_lacs_kathio

Mille Lacs Kathio State Park is a 9,000-acre state park located in central Minnesota. It includes several lakes, forests, and wetlands, providing habitat for a variety of bird species, including Bald Eagles, Great Blue Herons, and Pileated Woodpeckers. The park has several trails and observation decks that offer excellent birdwatching opportunities. Visitors can also fish, boat, and participate in educational programs and events.
