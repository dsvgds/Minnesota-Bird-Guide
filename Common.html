Common Birds In Minnesota

Minnesota is home to a diverse range of bird species, but some of the most common birds you're likely to encounter in the state include the American Robin, Black-capped Chickadee, Mallard Duck, Canada Goose, House Sparrow, Blue Jay, Northern Cardinal, and Mourning Dove, among others.

Black-capped Chickadee

The Black-capped Chickadee is a small, active bird with a distinctive black cap and white cheeks. They are common in Minnesota's forests and woodlands, and are known for their friendly and curious behavior.

Mourning Dove

The Mourning Dove is a common bird in Minnesota, with its soft, mournful call and graceful flight. They are often seen perched on wires or foraging on the ground for seeds.

Red-tailed Hawk

The Red-tailed Hawk is a large bird of prey that can often be seen soaring over fields and forests in search of prey. They have broad, rounded wings and a distinctive rust-colored tail. Red-tailed Hawks are skilled hunters, and their diet consists mainly of small mammals like rodents, rabbits and squirrels.

White-throated Sparrow

The White-throated Sparrow is a small, unobtrusive bird with a distinctive white throat and yellow markings above the eyes. They are found in wooded areas throughout Minnesota and are known for their clear, whistled song. White-throated Sparrows primarily feed on seeds and insects.

Eastern Bluebird

The Eastern Bluebird is a small, colorful bird with bright blue wings and rusty-red breast. They are found in open fields and woodland edges in Minnesota and are known for their gentle, melodious song. Eastern Bluebirds primarily feed on insects like caterpillars and beetles, but will also eat berries and other fruits.

Cedar Waxwing

The Cedar Waxwing is a sleek, crested bird with a distinctive mask of black feathers around the eyes. They are found in Minnesota's woodlands and suburban areas and are known for their high-pitched trilling call.

Common Grackle

The Common Grackle is a large, iridescent bird with a long, keel-shaped tail. They are found in urban and suburban areas throughout Minnesota and are known for their raucous calls and gregarious behavior.
