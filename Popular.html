Most Popular Birds

Minnesota is home to a wide variety of birds, but one of the most iconic and beloved species is the Common Loon, which is known for its distinctive calls, striking black-and-white plumage, and its presence on lakes and waterways throughout the state.

Common Loon

The Common Loon is a large, aquatic bird with striking black-and-white plumage, a red eye, and a haunting call that echoes across Minnesota's lakes. They are strong divers and can stay underwater for several minutes as they hunt for fish, their primary source of food. During breeding season, Common Loons can be seen performing a courtship dance where they swim around each other, dip their heads, and call to each other. Common Loons are an iconic bird of the north woods and have been designated as the state bird of Minnesota.

Bald Eagle

The Bald Eagle is a majestic bird of prey with distinctive white head and tail feathers and a powerful wingspan. They are a symbol of strength and freedom and can be found throughout Minnesota's lakes, rivers, and wetlands. Bald Eagles primarily feed on fish and can often be seen soaring high in the sky or perched on a tree branch overlooking the water. Once endangered, the Bald Eagle has made a remarkable recovery in recent decades and is now a common sight in many parts of Minnesota.

American Goldfinch

The American Goldfinch is a bright yellow bird with black wings and tail feathers, and a cheerful song that is often heard in Minnesota's gardens and backyards. They are social birds and can often be seen in flocks, feeding on sunflower seeds and other small seeds. During breeding season, the males will molt into a bright yellow plumage with a black cap, while the females remain a more subdued yellow-green. American Goldfinches are a common and welcome sight in Minnesota's gardens and parks.

Northern Cardinal

The Northern Cardinal is a bright red songbird native to North America, and it is a popular bird among birdwatchers and backyard bird enthusiasts. The male Northern Cardinal is known for its bright red plumage, while the female has a more muted brownish-red color with a distinctive crest on her head. Both males and females have a black mask around their eyes.They are a common sight in gardens, parks, and woodlands, and they are known for their beautiful songs, which are often described as sounding like "cheer-cheer-cheer" or "birdie-birdie-birdie."

Blue Jay

The Blue Jay is a bold and colorful bird with bright blue and white plumage and a distinctive call that is often heard in Minnesota's forests and suburbs. They are intelligent and curious birds, known to imitate other bird calls and even human speech. Blue Jays are omnivorous and feed on a wide variety of foods, including insects, seeds, and fruit. They are a common and recognizable bird in Minnesota's forests and suburbs.

Red-winged Blackbird

The Red-winged Blackbird is a medium-sized blackbird with a striking red and yellow shoulder patch on the male and a more subdued brown and white plumage on the female. They are a common sight in Minnesota's wetlands and grasslands, where they feed on insects and seeds. During breeding season, the male will defend his territory vigorously, puffing up his feathers and displaying his bright red and yellow patches to attract a mate.

Great Blue Heron

The Great Blue Heron is a tall and stately wading bird with blue-gray plumage, a long neck, and a sharp beak. They are commonly seen along Minnesota's lakes and streams, patiently stalking their prey in the water. Great Blue Herons are expert fishermen and can swallow fish whole, often seen swallowing a fish with a quick jerk of their head. They are a beautiful and graceful bird, often seen in flight with their slow, deliberate wingbeats.
