Minnesota birds

Minnesota is home to a rich and diverse avian community, with over 400 species of birds having been recorded in the state. From majestic bald eagles and colorful songbirds to elusive owls and rare waterfowl, Minnesota offers bird enthusiasts of all levels the opportunity to observe and learn about these fascinating creatures in their natural habitats. Whether you're a seasoned birder or a newcomer to the hobby, we hope you find the information and resources on this site helpful and informative. So grab your binoculars and join us as we explore the incredible world of Minnesota birds!

Conservation birds

Minnesota is home to a diverse range of bird species, but some of the most common birds you're likely to encounter in the state include the American Robin, Black-capped Chickadee, Mallard Duck, Canada Goose, House Sparrow, Blue Jay, Northern Cardinal, and Mourning Dove, among others.

Minnesota is home to a wide variety of birds, but one of the most iconic and beloved species is the Common Loon, which is known for its distinctive calls, striking black-and-white plumage, and its presence on lakes and waterways throughout the state.

Minnesota has several bird species that are in need of conservation and management, including the Greater Prairie Chicken, Sharp-tailed Grouse, Henslow's Sparrow, Upland Sandpiper, Piping Plover, and various waterfowl such as the threatened Trumpeter Swan and endangered Whooping Crane.

Minnesota is home to a rich variety of bird species and offers many birdwatching hotspots for enthusiasts to explore.
